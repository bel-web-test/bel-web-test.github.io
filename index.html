Wearable Bluetooth medical device

Hardware design, Firmware and Consulting.

This wearable device is a research platform to collect data and test embedded AI for medical detection.

This design includes 10 programmable ARM cores, 8 power rails, two Bluetooth radios, an IMU, data storage on eMMC and SD cards, Qi wireless charging and LiPo battery management into a miniature wearable design.

The HDI PCB was designed in concert with a specialist manufacturer to achieve world class results. The final layout included 12 layers with stacked microvias to allow for the 0.4mm WCSP and 0.4mm aQFN packages.

Firmware was developed for the device on-top of the Zephyr Project, an open source RTOS from the Linux Foundation. The firmware used an Event Stream Processing paradigm more commonly found in web micro-services. This is an example of one of the many ways in which we bring modern software engineering techniques to the traditionally hesitant world of embedded programming. The result was an extremely effective system.

The design was integrated into a larger system, requiring the implementation of multiple custom BLE services to manage the device remotely.

Technology highlights

  • Arm Cortex M33 (nRF53 and LPC55)
  • Bluetooth
  • LSM6 IMU
  • HDI PCB with stacked microvias
  • LiPo battery management
  • Zephyr (Embedded RTOS)
  • nRF Connect SDK
  • Custom BLE services
  • Qi Wireless charging

LoRaWan IoT Sensor node

Firmware Development, EMC support and RF consultancy.

This sensing device is used to detect premature failure of industrial installations. Raw data from the novel sensing technology is processed on device using “Edge AI” and extracted via a LoRaWAN. To ensure a long service life, the implementation is optimised for ultra low sleep current and extended sleep periods.

We were engaged to efficiently implement the system firmware needed by our client to deploy their nodes to trial sites. This provided a foundation on which the company and team could grow, allowing us to move to an on-going consultancy role as required.

As well as developing firmware, we provided an antenna measurement and matching service to ensure the best performance from the LoRaWAN module. We were able to increase the operational range by more than a factor of 4. Once the system was suitably functional, we accompanied it to EMC testing to ensure a smooth process.

  • LoRaWAN Backhaul
  • Antenna Matching
  • STM32 HAL
  • Cortex-M0 (STM32L0XX)
  • Ultra Low Power sleep state management
  • CMake, GCC, Docker, CI

Industrial Control of Water Purification Plant

Electronic Design, Manufacturing, Firmware Development.

Our client, a hydro-tech startup with a conscience is bringing clean water to developing nations. In the final stages of product development, their system will improve the quality of life for people across the planet.

We were engaged to design and develop the control electronics for the plant. This would be a highly cost optimised and rugged design. In order to fully specify the system and avoid over-specification, we designed and built two industrial control cabinets to act as instrumented prototypes. The flexibility of industrial modules allows for easy modification with changing requirements.

In addition to the process control required to run the plant, we provided remote telemetry of all process parameters via an LTE modem. The MQTT formatted data is monitored in the cloud with the capability to send alarm messages by SMS when human attention is required. Historic process data can be inspected with a web dashboard and by mobile app.

Technology highlights

  • DIN Rail mounted Industrial components
  • IoT monitoring of all process values
  • Containerised Embedded Linux
  • MQTT, CAN, Modbus, NTP
  • LTE Backhaul
  • Thingsboard backend
  • Bare Metal Firmware Development
  • STM32 HAL
  • Cortex-M7 (STM32F747)
